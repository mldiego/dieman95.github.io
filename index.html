About


I am a Research Scientist at the Institute for Software Integrated Systems at Vanderbilt University, where my work focuses on analyzing the safety and trustworthiness of AI, as well as leveraging generative AI to automatically develop safe and robust intelligent systems. During my tenure at Vanderbilt, I have been invited worldwide to teach tutorials about my work on assurance for deep learning models and its usage in security, cyber-physical systems, and medical image domains. I previously received a PhD in Electrical Engineering from Vanderbilt University under the supervision of Dr. Taylor T. Johnson. My research was focused on the development of assurance techniques for autonomous vehicles, which was demonstrated on an unmanned underwater vehicle and a 1/10 scale autonomous remote controlled car as part of the DARPA assured autonomy program.
